Articles


Convention/Grammar

Articles introduce nouns in English: the definite article "the" and indefinite articles "a" and "an".

"The" refers to specific or particular nouns, while "a" and "an" refer to non-specific or general singular countable nouns.

"A" is used before consonant sounds, and "an" before vowel sounds.


Aspect "A" "An" "The"
Type Indefinite Indefinite Definite
Usage Before singular countable nouns beginning with consonant sounds Before singular countable nouns beginning with vowel sounds Before specific or particular nouns (singular or plural)
Examples a book, a dog, a university an apple, an egg, an hour the sun, the Eiffel Tower, the childrene
Meaning Any “one” of a group Any “one” of a group A specific one or group
Special uses With certain expressions (e.g., "a few") With certain expressions (e.g., "an only child") With superlatives, ordinal numbers, unique things

Word Meaning

Stance: A way of standing or an attitude or position on an issue.
Sentence: He took a strong stance against bullying at school.

Nervous: Feeling worried, anxious, or scared about something.
Sentence: She felt nervous before her big test.

Groaning: Making a deep, long sound showing pain, discomfort, or disapproval.
Sentence: He was groaning in pain after he hurt his knee.
